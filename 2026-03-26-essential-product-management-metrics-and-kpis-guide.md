## Key Insights
- Product managers must define appropriate metrics and KPIs to assess product performance and ensure alignment with business objectives, fostering an evidence-driven culture.
- Metrics are fundamental for understanding customer needs and behaviors, identifying growth opportunities, and making data-driven product decisions.
- While all Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are metrics, not all metrics are KPIs; KPIs specifically measure performance over time.
- Key metric categories for product management include acquisition (e.g., CPA, bounce rate), engagement (e.g., DAU/MAU, session duration), retention (e.g., churn, LTV), and financial metrics (e.g., MRR, NRR).
